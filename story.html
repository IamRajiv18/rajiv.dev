The Story

How a curious kid with a laptop became someone who builds things for a living — and never wants to stop.

2021 — The Beginning
Chapter 01
It started with a blank page.

I remember the exact moment. A tutorial, a text editor, and a browser that somehow turned my words into a webpage. It was just Hello World in HTML — but that was enough. I was hooked.

I had no idea what I was getting into. No computer science background, no mentor, no roadmap. Just curiosity and a lot of tabs open.

"Every developer remembers the first time something they wrote actually worked. For me, it felt like discovering a superpower."

I started from scratch — HTML, then CSS, then the terrifying and wonderful world of JavaScript. I broke things constantly. I fixed them. I broke them again. That loop became my education.

✦
2022 — Going Deeper
Chapter 02
PHP became my first real obsession.

I picked up PHP and it changed everything. Suddenly I could build things that actually did something — forms that saved data, login systems, admin dashboards. The web felt alive for the first time.

I started with small projects and quickly got obsessed with making them better. Cleaner code, better UX, smarter backend. Each project taught me something new — MySQL queries, session handling, AJAX, the whole stack.

I also discovered that I genuinely loved design. Not just writing code, but thinking about how something feels to use. That combination — code AND design — became my thing.

✦
2023 — First Real Work
Chapter 03
I built my first production system.

In 2023, I graduated with my BCA and immediately went full-time on building. My first real project was a custom admin panel — role-based access control, audit logs, task calendar, the works. I built it end-to-end, deployed it, and watched real people use it.

That feeling is hard to describe. Knowing that something you made is running in the real world, with real data, helping real people. It's different from any project or tutorial. It made everything click.

"The best learning is building something that matters to someone. A tutorial teaches you syntax. A real project teaches you everything else."

✦
2024–2025 — Expanding
Chapter 04
Platforms, clients, and new frontiers.

I spent the next two years building full-scale platforms — Gauranga Consultancy, Chita State Medical Academy, Amur State Medical Academy. Each one taught me something new about scale, UX, and client collaboration.

I also started leaning heavily into security — AES-256 encryption, PBKDF2 key derivation, OWASP practices. I built a credential vault from scratch. I wrote Python automation scripts. I started learning Blender and video editing on the side.

Turns out I like making things look good as much as I like making them work.

✦
2026 — Now
Chapter 05
Building what I love.

Right now I'm doing three things: contract work, creative projects under @bash.rajiv, and slowly building my first indie game — Project Artist and Cat.

The game is something I've been dreaming about for years. It's 2D, narrative-driven, and completely hand-crafted. I'm doing the art, the code, the sound direction — everything. It's the hardest and most exciting thing I've ever worked on.

The story isn't over. Honestly, it feels like it's just getting started.
